Brand voice that's read the way you meant it.

Every reply your team sends is the brand talking. This guide is how I find a brand's voice and, just as important, match it to how the customer will actually read it.

Free to use · by Mónica Gómez Vesga
1

The inbox is the brand

A customer rarely meets your marketing team. They meet your support inbox, usually on a day when something went wrong. So for them, the voice in that reply is the brand. Brand voice in support isn't slang or jokes. It's the consistent set of choices in how you write: how warm or formal you are, how much energy you bring, the words you reach for and the ones you avoid.

Read the room before you pick a voice.

The same words can comfort one customer and insult another. A person who is upset reads a joke as mockery. So the first question is never “what's our brand voice?” It's “how is this person feeling right now?” Then you choose how much of the voice to bring.

4

Get each case to the right agent, fast

The fastest resolution starts before an agent even reads the ticket: by making sure it lands with the right person the first time. Every reassignment is lost minutes and a colder customer. Here's how I'd set that up in Zendesk.

1

Sort it the moment it lands

Triggers run on every new ticket and can auto-assign it to the right group, set its priority, and tag it, based on topic, channel, language, or sentiment. It starts in the right queue instead of bouncing around.

2

Route by skill, not just who's free

Skills-based routing sends a ticket to agents who can actually solve it (language, billing, technical), so the first person to open it is the right person. Fewer handoffs, faster fixes.

3

Let omnichannel routing balance the load

It auto-assigns tickets across email, chat, and calls by who is available and has spare capacity, so nothing sits unassigned and no single agent drowns.

4

Prioritize with SLAs and clean views

SLA policies push the most urgent and oldest tickets to the top, and well-built views make sure nothing slips while the team stays fast.

Route it right the first time and you cut the reassignments and reopens (the bounce), you speed up first response and resolution, and that shows up directly in CSAT. And because the voice lives in your macros, the customer gets the same calm, on-brand reply no matter who picks it up.

A note on plans: triggers, groups, and views work on every Zendesk plan; skills- and priority-based routing live on the higher tiers.
