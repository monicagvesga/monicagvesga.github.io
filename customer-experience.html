How I build a macro
  1. Spot the pattern. Group the tickets that keep repeating, like "where are my tickets?".
  2. Write one reply that fully solves it, on-brand, with variables for the personal bits.
  3. Build in the next question, so the customer doesn't have to write back a second time.
  4. Review and refine as wording or policy changes, so the whole team keeps sounding like one calm voice.

Done right, a macro like this turns a repeat question into a one-touch resolution (what Zendesk Explore counts as first-contact resolution).

Help center · Orders & returns

How do I return or exchange an item?

A self-serve help article. The job: answer the question completely, head off the obvious follow-ups, and still sound like a person wrote it.

Changed your mind, or something isn't quite right? No problem at all, here's exactly how it works.

You have 30 days from delivery to start a return:

  1. Open the return form and enter your order number (it's in your confirmation email).
  2. Tell us which item and why. That's how we catch anything that should really be a free replacement.
  3. We'll email you a prepaid label within one business day. Print it, pop the item in any sturdy box, and drop it at any carrier point.

Once it reaches us, your refund goes back to your original payment method within 3–5 business days, and we'll email you the moment it's on its way.

Want a different size or colour instead?

Choose exchange on the form and we'll send the new one as soon as the original is scanned at drop-off, so you're not left waiting twice.

Arrived damaged or faulty?

Skip the steps above and reply to your order email with a photo. We'll sort a replacement or full refund right away, and you won't pay for return shipping.

Past the 30 days?

Reach out anyway. If it's close, we'll usually still help.

Still stuck? Reply to any order email or start a chat, and a real person (often me) will pick it up.

Tickets that never arrived, the day before a booked visit

Hi Daniel, you're right to be frustrated. The day before your visit with no tickets is exactly the kind of stress no one should have, and I'm sorry the earlier emails slipped through. That part is on us.

Here's where things stand: I found your order (#48213, €240, confirmed) and just re-sent both tickets to this email. They should land within a few minutes, and I've attached them as a backup PDF too, so you're covered either way.

At the entrance, you can show either the email or the PDF; both carry the same barcode, so you're set. If anything looks off when you arrive, the front desk can scan your PDF on the spot, and you can reply to this email and we'll sort it straight away.

Again, sorry you had to chase this. Enjoy the visit, and once you're back, just reply here and I'll make the earlier silence right.

Live chat · Sensitive & intimate topics

An awkward question about a personal product

Intimate and personal-product brands live or die on how their support handles questions people are shy to ask. The job: normalise it, answer it clearly, zero shame, still completely professional.

Incoming · customer

hi… this is a bit awkward to ask 😅 i just got my order and i'm honestly not sure i'm using it right, or if it's meant to feel like that. also, can it be cleaned normally? sorry if this is tmi

Reply · Mónica

Hi! No need to apologise at all. These are exactly the questions we're here for, and you're really not the first to ask (or the hundredth 🙂). Let's get you sorted.

A couple of things that clear up most of it:

· It's working as intended if it feels gentle on the lowest setting and builds from there. Everyone's different, so start low and adjust until it feels right for you. There's no single 'correct' here.

· Cleaning: warm water and a little fragrance-free soap after each use, then let it air-dry fully before storing. The pouch it came in is perfect for keeping it somewhere cool and dry. Full care steps are in our care guide.

If something still feels off after that, tell me a bit more about what you're noticing and I'll help you work out whether it's normal or worth a replacement. Whatever it turns out to be, we'll make it easy, and keep it discreet.

Macros & templates · Tone at scale

Reusable replies that still feel personal

The systems behind fast support. Four templates I'd build into Zendesk, written so any agent can drop them in and still sound human. Variables in { }.

Refund approved

Hi {first_name}, good news: your refund of {amount} is approved and on its way back to your {payment_method} now. It usually lands within 3–5 business days, and you'll get an email the moment it clears. Anything else I can take off your plate while I'm here?

When I don't have the answer yet

Hi {first_name}, thanks for flagging this. I can see why it's frustrating, and I won't fob you off with a half-answer. Give me until {time} to check it properly with our {team} team and I'll come back with a real update, not a holding message. I've got it from here.

Saying no, kindly

Hi {first_name}, I wish I could say yes to {request}, I really do. We can't {action} because {reason}. What I can do instead is {alternative}, which gets you most of the way there. Want me to set that up?

Sensitive topic, opener

Hi {first_name}, thanks for trusting us with this. There's truly nothing awkward on our end, and I'm glad to help. {answer}. If anything's still unclear, just say the word and we'll keep going until it's fully sorted.
